acemoglu is a nobel prize winner

acemoglu won a nobel prize for: 1. something marxist economist paul baran said in the 1950s (settler vs non-settler colonialism). 2. using incorrect historical datasets. 3. telling us that "institutions are important," and that there are exactly two types of them: inclusive and extractive (meaning good or bad, generally). it must be said that this "institutionalist" (even though disgustingly binary) perspective is much better than the culturalist approach. congratulations acemoglu.
